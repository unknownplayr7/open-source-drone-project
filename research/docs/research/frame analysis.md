# Frame analysis

## Note that this is an overview

## 5 inches

### Uses

- FPV racing
- Freestyle flight
- Small camera drones

### Pros

- Cheapest parts
- Huge community support
- Compact and portable
- Mature ecosystem

### Cons

- Tight internal space
- Harder to add future equipment
- Lower flight time margin
- Smaller batteries

### Verdict

Great drone size in general, but likely too small for the project's final goals

## 6 inches

### Uses

- Long-range FPV
- Hybrid builds
- Experimental platforms

### Pros

- Better efficiency than 5"
- More space for electronics
- Easier 10–15 minute flights
- Still fairly compact

### Cons

- Smaller ecosystem than 5"
- Less common parts
- Some upgrade limits for MK4

### Verdict

May seem like a good choice at the start, but may hinder future versions

## 7 inches

### Uses

- Long-range missions
- Mapping
- Research drones
- Autonomous platforms

### Pros

- Excellent flight time
- Lots of room for upgrades
- Better payload capacity
- Easier to integrate GPS and telemetry
- Future-proof for final product

### Cons

- More expensive
- Larger and less portable
- Heavier batteries

### Verdict

Deemed best for the project, but space will be under-utilized for early versions

## H frame

### Uses

- Camera drones
- Experimental platforms
- Heavy-lift drones
- Specialized payload carriers

### Pros

- Large central body area
- Easier payload integration
- More room for wiring
- Better for bulky equipment

### Cons

- Slightly less aerodynamic
- Often heavier
- Less common today
- May have different flight characteristics front-to-back

### Verdict

Pros do not weigh out the cons

## X frame

### Uses

- FPV drones
- Long-range drones
- Research platforms
- Autonomous drones
- General-purpose quadcopters

### Pros

- Symmetrical flight characteristics
- Excellent maneuverability
- Efficient control algorithms
- Widely supported by flight software
- Large community and documentation
- Common in FPV and autonomous drones

### Cons

- Less internal space than H-frame
- Payload mounting can be difficult
- Electronics can become crowded

### Verdict

Seems like a good fit for less payload-centered drones
